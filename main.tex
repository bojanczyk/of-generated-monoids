\title{Which automata are monoids?}
\author{Miko{\l}aj Boja\'nczyk, Bartek Klin, and Jingjie Yang}

\maketitle 
\begin{abstract}
    In orbit-finite sets, deterministic automata are strictly stronger than monoids.
    %  For example, the language $\setbuild{ w \in \atoms^*}{the first letter of $w$ appears again in $w$}$ is recognised by a deterministic orbit-finite automaton, but not by an orbit-finite monoid.
      We give an algorithm which decides the difference, i.e.~it inputs a deterministic orbit-finite automaton, and decides whether it is equivalent to an orbit-finite monoid. 
\end{abstract}
